About

InRida is a ride-sharing mobile application designed to efficiently connect car owners and drivers. Users can register as either car owners or drivers, list vehicles for sharing, search for available rides, and manage their accounts seamlessly. The app leverages Flutter, Firebase, and Google Maps API for a modern, cross-platform experience.

Problem Statement

There is an increasing need for convenient and accessible transportation solutions. Existing ride-sharing platforms often lack flexibility for car owners to list vehicles and for drivers to easily find and rent them. InRida addresses this gap by providing a streamlined, role-based platform for both parties, with secure authentication and real-time data management.

Research goals
The goal was to create a seamless, efficient ride-sharing experience for both car owners and drivers. We focused on simplifying vehicle listing, ride searching, and account management, while ensuring robust security and real-time updates.
Application Use Cases

The InRida application supports three main user roles: Admin, Car Owner, and Driver. Each role has specific management and operational capabilities, as shown in the use case diagram.

Role-Based Flow

The flowchart illustrates the step-by-step process for car owners and drivers, from managing vehicle listings to handling rental requests and approvals. This ensures clear, role-based access and a streamlined workflow for all users.

UI/UX Design
The UI was designed in Figma for rapid prototyping and collaboration. The focus was on a clean, minimalistic, and role-based interface, ensuring a consistent and intuitive experience for both car owners and drivers.

Design Philosophy
InRida was built with modularity, scalability, and maintainability in mind. The architecture leverages the Provider pattern for state management, integrates Firebase for authentication and real-time data, and uses Google Maps for location-based features. The system is designed for rapid iteration and future extensibility.
Core Interactions
The core interactions are role-based, supporting car owners in managing vehicles and drivers in searching and booking rides. Real-time updates and intuitive navigation are central to the user experience.

CONCLUSIONS

_______   KEY FINDINGS

Measuring success.

"InRida was built to make ride-sharing seamless, secure, and accessible for everyone. The modular architecture and real-time features ensure a robust and scalable platform for the future."

What we did well

We successfully integrated Flutter, Firebase, and Google Maps to deliver a cross-platform, real-time ride-sharing experience. The Provider pattern made state management clean and scalable, and the UI/UX design ensured a smooth onboarding for both car owners and drivers.

What I would do differently

In future iterations, I would focus on expanding the payment system, improving onboarding for new users, and adding more advanced navigation features. Early user testing and feedback would also help refine the user experience further.
